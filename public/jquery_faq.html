U.S. National Park FAQs

How much land has been set aside for National Parks?
51 million acres! About the size of Kansas
What was the world's first National Park?
Yellowstone, created in 1872 by President Ulysses S. Grant. Cared for by the calvary.
What was the first cave to be delcared a National Park?
Wind Cave, the sixth longest cave in the world!
What is the smallest National Park?
Hot Springs National Park is only 5,500 acres!
How is a National Park chosen?
National Parks are chosen for their natural beauty, unique geological features, and unusual ecosystems.
What states do NOT have National Parks?
The only state without a National Park or Monument is the country's first state, Delaware.
What National Park has the lowest elevation?
Death Valley National Park, with 282 ft below sea level.
What National Park is considered a great mystery?
Scientists have debated whether the Grand Canyon was a gorge in which a river came to, or which it was carved by.
What National Park has the deepest water?
Crater Lake took 250 years of rain and snow accumulation to reach it's current level as U.S.' deepest lake!
What is the largest National Park?
Wrangell - St. Elias National Park in Alaska has an area of over 8.3 million acres and is larger than each of the nine smallest states.
